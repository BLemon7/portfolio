Monogram

Below you can see the monogram I designed based on my initials - BL. The overall shape is based on basic geometric shapes for a contemporary look. The colour scheme utilises purple to convey the mood of creativity and elegance on the first impression. I have created an all-black version for use against contrasting backgrounds. The two versions can be seen below.

Baskerville was responsible for numerous innovations in printing, paper and ink production. He developed a technique which produced a smoother whiter paper which showcased his strong black type. He also pioneered a completely new style of typography, adding wide margins and generous leading to improve legibility.

Word mark

Here is my word mark, which hasn't abstracted far from my monogram. I have added horizontal text as I think it is very effective for letterheads and website headers. The font choice is Exo which I have incorporated into my brand identity. I feel the sans type is effective at maintaining the contemporary style which I have employed in my monogram.

As with the monogram, I have created an all-black version for use against contrasting backgrounds. The two versions can be seen below.

Baskerville was born in the village of Wolverley, near Kidderminster in Worcestershire, and worked as a printer in Birmingham. Baskerville printed works for the University of Cambridge and, although an atheist, printed a splendid folio bible in 1763. His typefaces were greatly admired by Benjamin Franklin, a printer and fellow member of the Royal Society of Arts, who took the designs back to the newly-created United States, where they were adopted for most federal government publishing.
